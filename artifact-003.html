SPECACT REPORT TRANSCRIPT During the latest SPECACT deployment, a new anomalous structure was identified within the operational zone. The object appeared to be a large red monolithic stone suspended vertically within a deep crater-like formation surrounded by forest terrain. The structure emitted a visible red glow and contained unnatural geometric markings across its surface. No immediate physical or psychological effects were observed from direct visual exposure to the object itself.

The area surrounding the monolith contained multiple openings and holes leading into underground cave systems. Initial assessment suggests that the primary descent point from above may currently be the safest access route. The apparent main cave entrances at ground level are believed to be significantly more dangerous and possibly defended or monitored by hostile entities present in the area.

During exploration and movement around the anomaly, the SPECACT element encountered multiple unidentified hostile entities. These entities were almost entirely invisible, appearing primarily as black distortions or moving shadows. Contact with the entities resulted in operators being physically dragged toward them before being neutralized. The attacks were sudden, aggressive, and difficult to predict due to the limited visibility of the threats.

The entities demonstrated coordinated territorial behavior, especially near cave entrances and deeper sections of the area surrounding the monolith. Based on observations, it is highly likely that the underground cave network and the red structure are directly connected to the hostile activity within the zone.

Despite the hostile encounters, the red monolith itself did not display direct aggression or immediate anomalous influence on personnel during the operation. However, its presence, placement, and relation to the surrounding entities strongly suggest that it is either the source of activity within the area or an object being protected by the hostile forces.

The operation resulted in multiple losses after operators were overwhelmed by the entities during movement through the area. Standard engagement methods were ineffective due to the nature and speed of the hostile contacts. The environment itself appeared unstable and highly hazardous, particularly near cave access points.

Additionally, bodycam footage was successfully recovered from the operation. The footage contains visual evidence of the monolith, cave systems, operator movement, hostile encounters, and the behavior patterns of the entities. Further analysis of the recordings is strongly recommended for threat assessment, behavioral study, and future operational planning.

Overall assessment indicates that the area represents a high-threat anomalous zone containing:

• A previously unidentified red monolithic structure
• Extensive underground cave systems
• Multiple hostile invisible or shadow-like entities
• Potential territorial or protective behavior surrounding the anomaly

Further exploration should only be conducted with reinforced personnel, improved containment planning, and specialized equipment prepared for non-conventional hostile contact.
